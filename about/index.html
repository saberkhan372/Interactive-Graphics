Where this course came from

The course began from autobiography, but not as nostalgia. The story matters because it shows how access to machines, software, games, and visual tools arrives unevenly, and how each encounter leaves a different idea of what computing is for.

"Computer graphics is a vast topic with many areas worthy of investigation. Many of my first memories of computers are also about computer graphics and the impact it has on humans. The first few years of my life was completely without computers. I spent the first 8 years of my life in Bangladesh where I did not see a computer. I remember my father, an architect, rendering designs on AutoCAD on his Intel i386 PC in the late 1980s after we had moved to Saudi Arabia. Around that time my aunt in the United States sent us a Nintendo Entertainment System with a couple of games, Mario 3 and Duck Hunt. While the NES is not a full computer the 8-bit graphics and innovative game design got me hooked on video games."

"By the mid 1990s my family moved to the United States and I was able to get my first computer, a hand-me-down from my father. Inspired by 3D design and animation I began to play with software such as Bryce3D, a 3D modeling and rendering software... My hope is to provide a set of projects that will give you a strong foundation in the fundamentals of how 2D and 3D graphics are generated along with opportunities to show your creativity."

— Interactive Graphics welcome letter, September 8, 2022

The site's visual language — warm paper, quiet ink, era-authentic palettes — is documented in a living style guide, rendered from the same stylesheet every page uses.

Privacy

This site contains no student names, grades, or private feedback. Course logistics, roster contacts, and assessment data stay local and private. Where a lesson references real student work, it's described in the abstract or not at all unless separately reviewed and consented to.

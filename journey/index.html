The Course Journey

Two years of Interactive Graphics as they actually ran — real dates, real due days, real interruptions — drawn from the class logs and planners.

course moment project due guest & community

2022–2023 · The full arc

Semester 1: 38 classes in 14 weeks (one cancelled, one subbed) · Semester 2: 51 planned meetings in 19 weeks

  • September 8, 2022

    The welcome letter

    The course opens with autobiography: Bangladesh, an architect father rendering AutoCAD on an i386, an NES from an aunt, Bryce3D on a hand-me-down PC. Students write response letters about their own paths to computing.

  • September 22

    Pixel algorithms on paper

    Before any code: a design on graph paper, then a numbered, human-followable algorithm for recreating it. The whole course in miniature. Tool →

  • Fall 2022

    Processing Foundation visit

    Research into Dorothy Santos, Xiaowei Wang, and the Processing community — then first p5.js code in the web editor. Lesson →

  • October 13

    Class Logo due

    An original logo in Applesoft BASIC lo-res graphics, presented in class with a case for why it should be the class sticker. Assignment →

  • November 7

    BASIC Animation due

    The Unit 1 final: an animated 2D graphic in HGR mode with loops, sub-routines, and user input. Assignment →

  • December 5

    3D Wireframe due

    A complex model from simple 3D parts, rotating smoothly under user control — no WebGL, just the math. Assignment →

  • January 2023

    Semester 1, by the numbers

    38 classes · 14 weeks · 3 units · 3 projects · one cancelled class · one sub day
  • January 11

    Portfolio Site due

    Every project gets a public home: hand-built HTML and CSS on GitHub Pages. Assignment →

  • January 28

    CC Fest NYC at Dalton

    The free creative-coding festival comes to the school — keynotes from Christy Crawford (DOE CSforAll) and Dan Shiffman (NYU, Coding Train), workshops from educators across the city.

  • February 13

    3D Scene with WebGL due

    The big integrative build: primitives, custom shapes, textures, lights, camera, motion, interactivity. Assignment →

  • March 2

    GB Studio Central visits

    A virtual troubleshooting session with Emi from GB Studio Central, mid-project.

  • March 6

    Scene for Game Boy due

    An interactive GB Studio scene with original art, sound, and scripted elements — then Show & Tell. Assignment →

2023–2024 · The refinement

Planned as 40 classes in 19 weeks (30 with seniors) · 5 units · granular day-to-day planning

  • Fall 2023

    The course gets rebuilt

    Units renumbered and resequenced, with a class-by-class planner: warm-up questions, homework, and lesson notes for every meeting.

  • January 17–30

    Animating History with BASIC

    Research a real moment from graphics history, then bring it back to life on an Apple II emulator — research, outline, code, documentation, submission, on a published schedule. Assignment →

  • February 2 · Class 11

    The ChatGPT moment

    A generated comparison of Applesoft BASIC and p5.js appears in the lesson prep — kept in the course's public history as an object of critique, not an authority. How the course handles AI →

  • February 13

    Converting BASIC to p5.js due

    Students port their own BASIC project forward forty years. Same idea, new system. Assignment →

  • Spring 2024

    The pipeline unit lands

    The year's strongest addition: building a 3D wireframe renderer without WebGL, so the graphics pipeline is math students did themselves. Unit 3 →

  • April 26

    3D Simulation with WebGL due

    Real-time particles with velocity, momentum, and collisions — behavior, not just composition. Assignment →

2026 · The archive becomes a course again

  • July 2026

    This site

    Both years reconstructed as one public sequence: 34 lessons, 9 assignments, 10 interactive tools, and a teacher's guide — so the course can be browsed, taught, or remixed by anyone.

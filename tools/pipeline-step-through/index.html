Unit 3 — Pipeline and Wireframe

Pipeline Step-Through

A wireframe cube moves through the real graphics pipeline, one stage at a time — the same math every WebGL library does for you automatically, made visible.

The tool slows the pipeline down enough to make each coordinate system legible. The table shows what changes numerically; the cube shows what those changes mean visually.

Stages 0–3 are drawn with a fixed display angle so you can watch the cube change smoothly. Stage 4 (Screen Space) switches to the real perspective projection. That is the actual pipeline output, and it looks different on purpose.

Try this

Step through all five stages with the sliders left alone. Watch which numbers in the table change at each stage, and which stay the same.

Notice this

Notice that rotation only changes the numbers at World Space and after. Model Space is always the same cube, no matter what the sliders do.

Remix this

Push the field of view slider to its extreme in both directions at Screen Space. What does a very wide FOV do to the edges of the cube, and how does that compare to a wide-angle camera lens?

Unit 1 — BASIC and Fractals

Recursion Made Visible

A fractal tree draws itself while the call structure is counted beside it. Recursion becomes easier to read when you can see the repeated promise: draw one branch, then ask for two smaller branches.

Recursion is often taught as a mysterious idea, but the mechanism is plain: draw a branch, ask for two smaller branches, and stop when the base case is reached. The complexity comes from repetition, not from a more complicated rule.

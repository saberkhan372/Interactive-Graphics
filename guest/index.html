Guest Voices

Practitioners from outside the classroom who shaped how this course thinks about its material.

A course about graphics history should not rely only on the teacher's account of that history. Guest material gives students another source to read closely, question, and place beside the code they are writing.

Pixel Art as a Democratic Medium — MeenaKo

A history of pixel art across three technological eras: 1950s hardware constraint, 1980s and 1990s DIY web culture, and the 2000s casual-creator explosion. Written by professional pixel artist MeenaKo (moaw.art) as pre-reading before a Unit 0 guest visit, it covers Michael Noll and Shelley Lake, the invention of "art typing," Web 1.0 and Web 2.0, and the "Hi-Bit" era of pixel art defined by choice rather than hardware limits.

Class Logo in Lo-Res BASIC

Draw a logo — your initials, a class mark, anything readable — in Apple II lo-res graphics. GR mode gives you a 40 × 48 grid and a handful of commands: COLOR= to pick a color, HLIN for a horizontal line, VLIN for a vertical one. That's the whole toolbox. Pixels are expensive here, and the assignment is to feel that.

As assigned 2022–23, early Unit 1, on the Applesoft BASIC emulator.

The starter, as written in class

10 GR
11 REM LETTER I!
20 COLOR = 1
30 HLIN 1, 5 AT 1
40 VLIN 1, 5 AT 3
50 HLIN 1, 5 AT 6
60 REM Letter n!
70 VLIN 3, 6 AT 8
80 HLIN 7, 10 AT 4
90 VLIN 4, 6 AT 10

What to do

  • Sketch your logo on grid paper first — decide which lines carry each letterform;
  • Build it from HLIN and VLIN calls in GR mode;
  • Use at least two colors with COLOR=;
  • Comment each letter or section with REM.

Assignment · Unit 3

3D Wireframe

Build a complex model out of basic three-dimensional parts, and rotate it — smoothly, logically, all parts moving together. This is the assignment where the pipeline math stops being abstract: your shape, your vertices, your rotation.

As assigned 2022–23 · due Monday, December 5 · submit a GIF recording of the canvas plus a link.

Requirements

  • 500 × 500 canvas;
  • Use simple 3D shapes to construct a larger, complex model;
  • Use mouse or keyboard input to control the rotation of the model;
  • Rotations are smooth and logical;
  • All parts of the shape rotate together and appropriately;
  • Use saveGif() to record a GIF of the canvas showing rotation;
  • The model is centered on the canvas;
  • The code is well commented, including links to resources;
  • The code is well organized, with proper indentation and spacing;
  • Submit GIF and link.

Bonus

  • Use a non-cube, non-tetrahedron simple 3D shape;
  • Add colors to the surface of the wireframe;
  • Add an interesting distortion to the rotations.

How it ran

  • Paper design — a straightforward design of your 3D shape in your notebook;
  • Rough draft — a simple version in p5.js with all the necessary parts;
  • Final draft — finalize and submit the GIF and link.

Resources

  • Coding Train — 3D Rendering with Rotation and Projection
  • Khan Academy — 3D Shapes
  • Peter Collingridge — Rotation in 3D

Assignment · Unit 1

Animating History with BASIC

Pick a person, invention, or technique from the history of graphics, film, animation, or computer science — and bring it back to life in Applesoft BASIC. The research is half the work: you explain what you picked and why, with citations, then animate it on an emulator of the era's own hardware.

As assigned 2023–24 · due January 30. The teacher's own example: Tennis for Two, William Higinbotham's 1958 oscilloscope game — perhaps the first computer game in history.

Directions

  • Pick a person, invention, or technique from the history of graphics, film, animation, or computer science to illustrate using BASIC;
  • Explain the person/invention/technique and why you picked it, with citation, in a doc;
  • Write code in the Applesoft BASIC emulator and copy it into the doc;
  • Animate to show action and/or functionality — screen-capture the result;
  • Submit the doc with edit access and upload the screen capture.

How it ran

  • Research — select a topic (January 17);
  • Preparation — gather information, outline on paper (January 19);
  • Coding — write the BASIC code to animate the topic (January 23–24);
  • Documentation — document the project and explain the topic (January 26);
  • Finalization and submission (January 30).

Rubric — 10 points

Assignment · Unit 4 · Major Project

3D Scene with WebGL

Create an interactive 3D scene using WebGL in p5.js. This is the course's big integrative build: everything Unit 4 isolated one at a time — primitives, custom geometry, textures, lights, materials, camera — composed into one scene of your own design, with motion and interactivity.

As assigned 2022–23 · due Monday, February 13 · submit a GIF or movie plus a link to the project.

Requirements

  • Use translate() and rotate() to place objects;
  • Import a 3D object into the scene from a file;
  • Adjust camera and view — for example with camera(), perspective(), and/or ortho();
  • Use multiple 3D primitive shapes — box(), plane(), sphere(), ellipsoid(), cone(), cylinder(), torus();
  • Create custom 3D shape(s) using beginShape(), vertex(), and endShape();
  • Use an image to add texture to multiple 3D shapes with texture();
  • Adjust light and material to fit the scene — directional, ambient, or point light; normal, ambient, or specular material;
  • One or more objects in motion;
  • Add interactivity so the user can move the camera and/or objects;
  • Add the sketch to a new page on your portfolio website.

Bonus

  • Add interactivity between objects — collisions, texture, or shape change;
  • Integrate lightFalloff(), emissiveMaterial(), specularColor(), spotLight(), noLights(), frustum();
  • Integrate p5.EasyCam, a p5.js library for easy 3D camera control.

How it ran

  • Paper design / research — a straightforward paper design of your scene in your notebook;
  • Rough draft — a simple version in p5.js with all the necessary parts;
  • Final draft — finalize and submit the GIF and link.

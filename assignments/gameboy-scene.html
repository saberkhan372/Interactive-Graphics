Assignment · Unit 5

Scene for Game Boy

Create an interactive scene for the Game Boy using GB Studio. After a semester of writing code directly, this unit hands you a no-code engine and a 1989 handheld's constraints — four shades of green, 8×8 tiles — and asks what you can make matter inside them.

As assigned 2022–23 · due Monday, March 6 · submit a link to the project folder plus a screenshot. Mid-project: a virtual visit from Emi at GB Studio Central.

What the scene is graded on

  • Originality and creativity — a unique concept that goes beyond the basics, with your own creative touches;
  • Sound — appropriate sounds that enhance the scene, especially during interactions;
  • Functionality — the scene works properly and runs bug-free;
  • Gameplay — easy to understand and fun to play;
  • User interface — controls are explained, easy to use, responsive;
  • Scene design — visually appealing and organized;
  • Full use of elements — player sprite, non-player actors, background tiles, triggers and obstacles;
  • Custom art or animation — create and use an original sprite, background, or animation;
  • Custom scripted or coded elements — add new script or code to the project;
  • Unique 2D/2.5D/3D perspective — go beyond conventional top-down or platformer movement: orthographic tricks, sprite stacking, camera logic;
  • Add the scene to a new page on your portfolio website.

Bonus

  • Power-ups — a health boost that restores the player, a speed boost that quickens movement;
  • Hidden secrets — a collectible in a hard-to-reach spot, a hidden room behind a puzzle;
  • Alternate endings — finish without damage for a special ending, or branch the path.

How it ran

  • Paper design / research — February 27;
  • Rough draft — the simplest working version of your scene — February 28;
  • Troubleshooting — virtual visit from Emi at GB Studio Central — March 2;
  • Final draft — submit, then Show & Tell in class — March 6.

Unit 2

2D to 3D

This is the hinge between flat drawing and spatial thinking. The course does not begin 3D with a complete scene; it begins with a shape students already understand, gives that shape depth, and asks what new information the drawing must now carry.

The shortest unit and the most important hinge: everything up to now was x and y. Now there is a z. This unit treats that addition as a real event. A shape that already exists gets a third dimension added to it, live, so the jump is felt rather than just declared.

Covers: intro to WebGL in p5.js, making 3D shapes, making new 3D shapes, and porting a 3D rectangle example from Applesoft BASIC into JavaScript. It is the same translation exercise from Unit 1, one dimension further along.

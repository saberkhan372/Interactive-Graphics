Unit 4 · Lesson 4.4

Light and Materials

Light and material appear together on the surface, which makes them easy to confuse. This lesson separates the source of light from the surface response so students can see which command causes which change.

Source deck: IG 3.4 - Light & Materials.pptx · 5 slides

What the class did

The warm-up asked what p5.js WebGL can do, which commands and shapes students already have, and what can be adjusted. The lesson then introduced the functions that control surface and illumination: ambientMaterial, normalMaterial, fill, ambientLight, directionalLight, and pointLight.

The conceptual work was to stop treating a lit object as a single visual effect. The shape, its material, and the light acting on it are different parts of the scene.

Code shown

ambientLight(60);
directionalLight(255, 255, 255, -1, 0, -1);
ambientMaterial(220, 120, 80);
sphere(90);

Key idea

Lighting describes how the scene is illuminated. Material describes how the object responds. A good 3D scene needs both.

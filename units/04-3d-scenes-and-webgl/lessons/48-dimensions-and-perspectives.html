Unit 4 · Lesson 4.8

Dimensions and Perspectives

Projection vocabulary gives students a way to talk about how a 3D scene becomes a 2D image. Perspective, orthographic, axonometric, isometric, dimetric, and trimetric are not only drawing styles; they are different answers to the same flattening problem.

Source deck: IG 3.8 - Dimensions and Perspectives.pptx · 4 slides

What the class did

The deck's useful public material is its vocabulary: projection, perspective projection, parallel projection, vanishing point, orthographic projection, axonometric projection, trimetric, dimetric, and isometric. This lesson names the projection choices students will see again in the pipeline and camera work.

The course uses the vocabulary as a bridge between drawing, WebGL, and game graphics. A 3D scene is always being flattened for a screen; the question is what kind of flattening the system chooses.

Key idea

Projection is not a neutral final step. It decides how depth, scale, parallel lines, and vanishing points will be represented on a flat display.
